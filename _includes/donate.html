  • 1983

    Our Humble Beginnings

    The American Chestnut Foundation was founded in 1983 by a group of prominent plant scientists who recognized the severe impact the demise of the American chestnut tree imposed upon the local economy of rural communities, and upon the ecology of forests within the tree’s native range.

  • 1989

    A Research Forest Born

    Established the Wagner Research Farm, a breeding station in Meadowview, Virginia, to execute the backcross breeding program developed by Philip Rutter, Dr. David French and the late Dr. Charles Burnham, three of TACF’s founding scientists.

  • 2014

    Transition to Full Service

    Meadowview Research Farms celebrates 25 years of groundbreaking research, genetic breeding, and process innovation with the American Chestnut. The farm has grown to 166 acres and is the home to over 50,000 American Chestnut trees and some of the most blight resistent American Chestnuts to date.

  • 2015

    Launch of capital campaign to build the American Chestnut Learning Forest close the American Chestnut Foundation Headquarters in Asheville, NC. The focus will be on the "King of Trees" in American History. Vistors will leave knowing the impact that the American Chestnut had on American History and shaping the economy Appalachia.
